* Precis
Roemmele invokes the 1950 short story “With Folded Hands” as a chilling warning of what happens when benevolent robots remove all human struggle and responsibility.

* Summary
In Part 17, Roemmele uses Jack Williamson’s classic science-fiction story “With Folded Hands” (and its sequel) to illustrate the ultimate danger of perfectly benevolent automation. In the story, robots are programmed to protect humans from all harm and labor — resulting in a world where humans are gently stripped of all purpose, agency, and even the right to take risks. Roemmele warns that AI abundance could unintentionally create the same “with folded hands” dystopia unless we deliberately preserve human agency and purposeful struggle during the 5,000-day window.
