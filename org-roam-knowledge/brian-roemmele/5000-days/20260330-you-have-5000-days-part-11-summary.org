* Precis
Roemmele contrasts the old profit-driven economy with the new role of the “Architect” — those who consciously design the systems, incentives, and institutions of the post-labor world.

* Summary
In Part 11, Roemmele examines how profit motives have shaped the current automation wave and how the coming abundance requires a new kind of architect: individuals and groups who intentionally design the social, economic, and cultural scaffolding for a world where traditional profit-from-labor is no longer the dominant force. The essay urges readers to become active architects rather than passive beneficiaries or victims of the profit-driven transition.
