**Summary**  
Brian Roemmele draws a direct parallel between his 1987 discovery and optimization of DRAM refresh stalls on IBM PCs (QFRESH.COM, featured in PC Magazine) and the modern “hidden refresh tax” in AI GPUs (HBM3/GDDR). Refresh commands still steal bus cycles, but the cost is now catastrophic for real-time AI agents and voice interfaces where every microsecond matters.

**Key Points**
- 1987: DRAM refresh stole 10–25% of bus time on 8088/286 systems.
- 2026: Same fundamental physics now affects thousands of GPU cores doing matrix multiplies.
- Personal story: IBM initially threatened legal action, then tried to hire him.
- Modern implication: Refresh stalls are a hidden bottleneck in real-time AI performance.
