## Summary
Brian Roemmele uses the 1957 romantic comedy *Desk Set* (Katharine Hepburn, Spencer Tracy, and an early IBM “electronic brain”) as a prophetic mirror for our current AI-driven Interregnum. The film eerily foreshadows today’s hype cycles, mass automation fears, pink-slip panic, and the recurring debate about whether machines will replace human workers. Roemmele argues that, just as in 1957, humans remain essential — the real challenge is navigating the transition intelligently rather than succumbing to fear or naive utopianism. This part of the 5000 Days series turns an old Hollywood movie into a practical battle plan for the next decade of work and society.
