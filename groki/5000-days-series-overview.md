## Core Thesis
Brian Roemmele’s *5000 Days* series is a long-form exploration of the current technological Interregnum — the turbulent transition period between the old industrial economy and the emerging AI-driven world. The series argues that we are roughly halfway through a 10–15 year period of massive disruption (roughly 2020–2035) and that **how we navigate the next 5000 days will determine the trajectory of human civilization for the rest of the century**.

## Major Themes Across the Series
- **Historical Pattern Recognition**: Repeated cycles of automation fear, hype, and adaptation (Desk Set Prophecy in Part 25 being a prime example).
- **The End of Work as We Know It**: Traditional jobs disappearing, but new forms of human value and meaning emerging.
- **Human-AI Symbiosis**: Humans remain essential; the real question is whether we augment or abdicate our agency.
- **Memory & Collective Intelligence**: The danger of civilizational amnesia and the necessity of robust long-term knowledge systems.
- **Practical Navigation**: Concrete strategies, mindset shifts, and tools needed to thrive in the coming decade.

## Key Parts Ingested So Far
